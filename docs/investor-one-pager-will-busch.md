# Investor One-Pager — Will Busch

**Last updated:** July 14, 2026
**Operating base:** Azusa, California, US. Living at home, zero fixed costs, no dependents. US tax resident — all trading in a taxable brokerage account, so realized gains are taxed as short-term ordinary income unless held 12+ months.

---

## North Star

I'm 22 with a $45k account, no rent, no obligations, and a coaching gig that covers my food. My only real asset is time — I have decades of compounding runway, and that runway is the thing I'm actually protecting, not the balance. I buy quality large- and mid-cap tech when the chart is clean and the tape has temporarily broken it, and I hold for months while the market catches up. Aggressive stays on until I have real obligations — family, mortgage, someone depending on me. Losing money at 22 costs me an account. Losing the *runway* costs me the whole thing.

## Core Philosophy

- **Buy fear in quality, not in garbage.** Support levels and oversold RSI are the trigger, but the company has to be a real business — large-cap tech, or mid-cap tech with a clean chart. Weakness in a good company is opportunity. Weakness in a bad one is information.
- **Concentration is the edge, not diversification.** 3–5 names. If I can't explain why a position deserves a quarter of my book, it doesn't belong in the book.
- **Time in the position is the real leverage.** Months-to-a-year holds. LEAPs, not weeklies. I want to be leveraged to being *right*, not to being right *by Friday*.
- **The scale-in is planned, not emotional.** Adding on weakness is my highest-conviction move — which means it has to be the most rule-bound one. Three tranches, mapped before entry. After tranche three, the position is closed for adds. Permanently.
- **Trims go to cash, not to losers.** Selling strength to feed a name I'm already down on converts my winners into my losers. If I trim, the proceeds sit in cash until a *new* setup clears my entry criteria on its own merits.
- **Cash is a position.** I cannot buy the crash I claim to be waiting for if I'm 95% deployed when it arrives. 10% floor, always.
- **Averaging down has been rewarded, not validated.** Every time I've bought into a falling name, the market bailed me out. That's a bull market handing me a trophy for a process that hasn't been tested. I am not confusing the two.

## Time Horizon

**Core (months to a year):** where nearly everything lives. Quality tech bought at technical extremes, held while mean reversion does its work. This is the book.

**Tactical (weeks):** does not currently exist, and shouldn't. With 1–5 hrs/week of screen time, I have no business running a short-horizon book. If I ever want one, it gets its own capital allocation and its own rules — not a raid on the core.

**Generational (decade+):** the entire account, functionally. At 22 with no expenses, every dollar in here is compounding capital. The books stay psychologically separate by one rule: **no drawdown is a "long-term hold" unless it was a long-term hold before it went down.** Reclassifying a broken trade as an investment is the lie I'm most likely to tell myself.

## Mindset Rules

1. **Max 25% per name. Shares and LEAPs on the same underlying count as one bucket.** ORCL stock plus ORCL calls is one position, not two.
2. **LEAP sleeve caps at 25% of total book.** Currently 33%. Leveraged conviction on top of concentrated conviction is the same bet counted twice.
3. **Three tranches per name, maximum.** Map the levels before the first buy. Discretion on *when*, never on *how many*.
4. **After tranche three, the name is closed for adds — no exceptions, no matter how good it looks.** This rule exists specifically for the moment I most want to break it.
5. **Trim a winner → the money goes to cash.** It does not go to the position I'm underwater on. Ever.
6. **Never hold less than 10% cash.** The opportunity I'm waiting for is worthless if I'm out of ammunition when it shows up.
7. **"A better opportunity" is not an exit.** It is a rationalization for moving on from a loss without admitting it was one.
8. **Write the invalidation level before the entry, or don't take the entry.** Not a prediction. Insurance.
9. **Sentiment on X and Discord is a data point, never a trigger.** If I can't defend the trade from my own chart, I don't have a trade.

## Personal Nuances

- **Income & liquidity:** ~$250/week coaching, fully consumed by food. Zero inflows to the account. The $45k is a **finite, non-replenishable pot** — every dollar lost is permanently gone. "Who cares if I lose money, I don't pay for anything" is emotionally true and financially false.
- **Tax:** Taxable account. My months-to-a-year hold period sits right on the short-term/long-term line. Holding past 12 months is worth real basis points; check the clock before selling a winner in month 10.
- **Time budget:** 1–5 hrs/week. This rules out day trading, short-dated options, and anything requiring active management. LEAPs and multi-month holds are the honest instrument for the time I actually have.
- **Blind spot — buying too early.** My single most expensive habit, self-identified. My entries are right on thesis and wrong on timing. The scale-in ladder exists to survive my own impatience.
- **Blind spot — the bailout illusion.** Averaging down has always worked for me. It has never been tested in a real bear market. I am one regime change away from finding out.
- **Blind spot — no invalidation.** Asked point-blank for the price at which I'd admit I was wrong on my largest position, my honest answer was: *there is no such price.* That is stated here, out loud, because it is the thing most likely to cost me the account.
- **Behavioral trigger:** A stock breaking support while I'm already in it. My reflex, verified across three separate stress tests, is *buy more.* Every time. The rules above exist because that reflex has no natural off switch.
- **Family:** No constraints, no dependents, no partner input. Full autonomy — which means no external circuit breaker either. I am my own risk manager, and I am not yet good at it.

## Anti-Portfolio

- **Penny stocks, sub-$1.** No liquidity, no floor, no chart worth reading.
- **Meme stocks.** The thesis is other people's emotions. I can't chart that.
- **Crypto.** Not my circle of competence, not my edge, not my capital.
- **Short-dated and 0DTE options.** With 1–5 hrs/week, buying theta decay is buying a losing race against a clock I'm not watching.
- **Any position where I cannot write the invalidation level before I enter.** If I can't name the price where I'm wrong, I don't own a thesis — I own a hope.
- **Any add beyond tranche three.** Regardless of how oversold, how cheap, or how "generational" it looks.
